For one last week (I'm not THAT evil), I am asking you to compute the arithmetic mean, geometric mean, and harmonic mean (hint: the solutions to those problems will be available before this is due).

The computeMean function has 4 parameters: an array of doubles, an int specifying the length of the array, and two pass-by-pointer parameters of type double. Complete this function so that it "returns" the harmonic and geometric means via the pass-by-pointer parameters and really returns (e.g., using a return statement) a double equal to the arithmetic mean of the array's entries. Write your main() function so that it creates 2 arrays of doubles: one with a length of 2 and one with a length of 3. Read in 2 numbers into the first array and call computeMean. Print out the arithmetic mean, geometric mean, and harmonic mean that computeMean calculated (make sure you print out a space after each number). Read in 3 numbers into your second array, use it in a call to computeMean and, print out the arithmetic, geometric, and harmonic means calculated by that 2nd call (again with a space after each). MAKE SURE ALL 6 NUMBERS ARE PRINTED ON THE SAME LINE WITH A SPACE AFTER EACH ONE
